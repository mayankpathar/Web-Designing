Welcome to Incradible Gujarat

  • Gujarat is a state located on the western coast of India, known for its rich history, culture, heritage, and economic development. It was formed on 1st May 1960 after being separated from the Bombay State.

  • The state shares its borders with Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and the Arabian Sea. Gujarat has the longest coastline among all Indian states, stretching over 1,600 kilometers.

  • The capital city of Gujarat is Gandhinagar, while Ahmedabad is the largest city and an important cultural and economic center. Ahmedabad is also recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage City.

  • Gujarat is known as the birthplace of great leaders such as Mahatma Gandhi and Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, who played major roles in India’s freedom struggle and national unity.

  • The state has a strong and diverse economy based on industries such as textiles, petrochemicals, pharmaceuticals, dairy, agriculture, and diamond processing. Gujarat is one of the most industrialized and economically advanced states in India.

  • Gujarat is famous for its vibrant culture, traditional clothing, folk dances, and festivals. Popular dances include Garba and Dandiya Raas, especially celebrated during the festival of Navratri.

  • The cuisine of Gujarat is unique and widely appreciated for its variety of vegetarian dishes. Famous foods include Dhokla, Thepla, Khandvi, Undhiyu, Fafda, Jalebi, and many other traditional delicacies.

  • The state is home to several famous tourist attractions such as the Statue of Unity (the world’s tallest statue), Gir National Park (home of Asiatic lions), Rann of Kutch, Somnath Temple, and Dwarka.

  • Gujarat has a strong agricultural base, producing crops like cotton, groundnut, wheat, rice, sugarcane, and spices. It is also known for dairy development through the Amul Cooperative Movement.

  • The state promotes renewable energy and is a leader in solar and wind power generation in India, contributing to sustainable development.

  • Gujarati is the official language of the state, and the people of Gujarat are known for their entrepreneurship, hospitality, and business skills across the world.

  • Overall, Gujarat is a state that beautifully combines ancient traditions with modern development, making it one of the most important and progressive regions of India.


Historical & Cultural Places of Gujarat

Somnath Temple

Somnath is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India, famous for the Somnath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. It is located on the Arabian Sea coast and is known for its spiritual importance and rich history.

Dwarka

Dwarka is an ancient city and a major Hindu pilgrimage center, believed to be the kingdom of Lord Krishna. The Dwarkadhish Temple is the main attraction, drawing devotees from all over India.


Nature & Wildlife of Gujarat

Rann of Kutch

The Rann of Kutch is a vast white salt desert located in Gujarat. It is famous for the Rann Utsav, cultural festivals, handicrafts, folk music, and its stunning landscape, especially during the full moon.

Gir National Park

Gir National Park is the only natural habitat of Asiatic lions in the world. It is rich in wildlife and vegetation, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.


Beaches of Gujarat

Mandvi Beach

Mandvi Beach is a peaceful and scenic beach near Kutch, known for its clean shoreline, camel rides, and sunsets. It also has historical importance due to Mandvi Port and Vijay Vilas Palace nearby.

Dwarka Beach

Dwarka Beach is located near the Dwarkadhish Temple and offers a calm environment with beautiful views of the Arabian Sea. It is a popular spot for relaxation and evening walks.


Culture & cities

Junagadh

Junagadh is a historic city at the foothills of Girnar Hills, famous for ancient forts, palaces, and temples. It reflects a blend of Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic heritage.

Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is the largest city in Gujarat and a major cultural and economic center. It is known for its Sabarmati Ashram, historic architecture, textile industry, and delicious street food.

Bhuj

Bhuj is a historic city in the Kutch district of Gujarat, known for its rich culture, heritage, and handicrafts. It serves as the gateway to the Rann of Kutch and is famous for attractions like Aina Mahal, Prag Mahal, Bhujodi village, and traditional Kutchi art and embroidery. Bhuj also reflects a unique blend of history, royal architecture, and vibrant local traditions.
